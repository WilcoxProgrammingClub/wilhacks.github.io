About The Event

WilHacks 4.0 is an in-person 24 hour hackathon run by Wilcox High School. Work in teams of up to four to create unique projects and win cool prizes! We encourage and welcome any high school student to participate, regardless of programming experience.

F.A.Q

  • What is the goal of a hackathon?

    The goal of a hackathon is to create fully functioning software by the end of the event. The software can be a website, an app, a game, and so on.

  • Are there any prerequisites or required skills?

    While coding and programming is a huge plus, teams will also need people with excellent ideas and strong presenting skills.

  • Who can take part in the hackathon?

    The hackathon is open to any high school students or home schoolers in the equivalent grade.

  • How will teams work?

    Teams can be between 1-4 people. There will also be an opportunity to meet new people and form teams during the hackathon .

  • When will the hackathon take place?

    The hackathon will take place from Friday March 31st to Saturday April 1st. It will be 24 hours long, including three hours of judging time. There will be 21 hours to create a project starting at 9pm PST on Friday and ending at 6pm PST on Saturday. Judging will start at 6pm PST on Saturday, and end at 9pm PST.

  • How much does it cost?

    The hackathon is completely free to attend!

  • Are there prizes?

    There are various prizes for various categories. You can view them on our prizes section

  • Who will be judging?

    Typically, we have people from the industry come judge hackathon projects. The judges will be announced closer to the date of the hackathon.
